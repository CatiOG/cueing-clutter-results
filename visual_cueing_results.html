Note on Accuracy: Raw hit rate was not used as a primary dependent variable in the analyses reported here. Unlike accuracy, which does not distinguish between perceptual discriminabilty and response bias, sensitivity (d) accounts for both hits and false alarms. This yields a more accurate reflection of a participants actual detection capacity. Accuracy is shown above for transparency; the consistently high values confirm that the observed d differences reflect genuine detection improvements rather than floor or ceiling artefacts.
